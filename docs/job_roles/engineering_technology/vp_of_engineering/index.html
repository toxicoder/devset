VP of Engineering

Role Code: EXEC0003

Job Description

An executive leadership role responsible for the overall engineering strategy, culture, and execution within the organization. The VP of Engineering leads the engineering management team, ensuring that products are built on time, within budget, and to high quality standards. They align technical goals with business objectives, manage the engineering budget, and foster a culture of innovation and psychological safety. They are the bridge between the C-suite and the engineering department.

Responsibilities

  • Engineering Strategy: Define and execute the long-term technical vision and roadmap in alignment with company goals. You will decide on key technology investments (e.g., cloud migration, AI adoption) and architectural standards. You ensure the engineering organization is positioned to support future business growth. You anticipate industry shifts and adapt the strategy accordingly.
  • Team Leadership and Management: Manage and mentor Directors of Engineering and Engineering Managers. You will define the organizational structure, hiring plans, and career ladders for the department. You conduct performance reviews for your direct reports and handle high-level personnel issues. You act as a coach, helping leaders grow into their roles.
  • Culture Building: Foster a positive, inclusive, and high-performance engineering culture. You will promote values like psychological safety, continuous learning, and blameless post-mortems. You drive initiatives for diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI) within the hiring pipeline. You ensure that engineers feel valued and motivated.
  • Operational Excellence: Oversee the software development lifecycle (SDLC) and ensure efficient delivery processes. You will monitor key engineering metrics (DORA metrics) like cycle time, deployment frequency, and change failure rate. You remove systemic blockers that hinder productivity. You ensure the team balances feature work with technical debt reduction.
  • Budget Management: Manage the engineering department‚Äôs budget, including headcount, software licenses, cloud infrastructure costs, and vendor contracts. You will negotiate with vendors to get the best value for the company. You allocate resources effectively across different teams and projects. You justify budget requests to the CFO.
  • Cross-Functional Collaboration: Collaborate closely with Product, Design, Sales, and Marketing leaders to ensure engineering is aligned with business needs. You will manage stakeholder expectations regarding timelines and deliverables. You participate in executive meetings to represent the engineering perspective. You break down silos between departments.
  • Talent Acquisition and Retention: Oversee the recruiting strategy to attract top engineering talent. You will define the employer brand for engineering. You implement retention strategies to keep high performers, such as competitive compensation and growth opportunities. You ensure the interview process is rigorous and fair.
  • Technical Governance: Establish and enforce engineering standards, security policies, and compliance requirements. You will ensure that the organization adheres to best practices for data privacy (GDPR, CCPA) and security (SOC 2). You manage technical risk and ensure disaster recovery plans are in place. You have the final say on major architectural decisions.
  • Innovation: Encourage experimentation and the adoption of new technologies that can provide a competitive advantage. You will sponsor hackathons and R&D initiatives. You create space for engineers to explore new ideas. You balance innovation with stability.
  • Communication: Communicate engineering progress, challenges, and wins to the rest of the company. You will translate technical concepts into business language for non-technical stakeholders. You hold All-Hands meetings to keep the engineering team informed and aligned. You are the face of engineering.

Role Variations

  • CTO (Chief Technology Officer): In smaller companies, the VP of Engineering and CTO roles might be combined. In larger ones, the CTO focuses on technical vision and R&D, while the VP of Engineering focuses on people, process, and execution. The VP of Eng reports to the CTO or CEO.
  • Head of Engineering: A title often used in mid-sized startups. Similar responsibilities to a VP but might be more hands-on with code or architecture initially. They scale the team from 10 to 50 engineers.
  • Director of Engineering: A level below VP, typically managing a specific vertical (e.g., Platform, Product) or a group of teams. The VP manages multiple Directors.
  • VP of Platform Engineering: Focuses specifically on the internal infrastructure and developer tools teams. They treat the rest of engineering as their customer.
  • VP of Product Engineering: Focuses specifically on the teams building customer-facing features. They work very closely with the CPO.

Common Partners

  • CTO (Chief Technology Officer): Aligns on technical vision and R&D.
  • CPO (Chief Product Officer): Collaborates on product roadmap and delivery.

  • Director of People/HR: Partners on hiring, retention, and performance management.
  • CFO (Chief Financial Officer): Collaborates on budget and resource allocation.
