# VP of Engineering

**Role Code:** EXEC0003

## Job Description
An executive leadership role responsible for the overall engineering strategy, culture, and execution within the organization. The VP of Engineering leads the engineering management team, ensuring that products are built on time, within budget, and to high quality standards. They align technical goals with business objectives, manage the engineering budget, and foster a culture of innovation and psychological safety. They are the bridge between the C-suite and the engineering department.

## Responsibilities

*   **Engineering Strategy:** Define and execute the long-term technical vision and roadmap in alignment with company goals. You will decide on key technology investments (e.g., cloud migration, AI adoption) and architectural standards. You ensure the engineering organization is positioned to support future business growth. You anticipate industry shifts and adapt the strategy accordingly.
*   **Team Leadership and Management:** Manage and mentor Directors of Engineering and Engineering Managers. You will define the organizational structure, hiring plans, and career ladders for the department. You conduct performance reviews for your direct reports and handle high-level personnel issues. You act as a coach, helping leaders grow into their roles.
*   **Culture Building:** Foster a positive, inclusive, and high-performance engineering culture. You will promote values like psychological safety, continuous learning, and blameless post-mortems. You drive initiatives for diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI) within the hiring pipeline. You ensure that engineers feel valued and motivated.
*   **Operational Excellence:** Oversee the software development lifecycle (SDLC) and ensure efficient delivery processes. You will monitor key engineering metrics (DORA metrics) like cycle time, deployment frequency, and change failure rate. You remove systemic blockers that hinder productivity. You ensure the team balances feature work with technical debt reduction.
*   **Budget Management:** Manage the engineering department's budget, including headcount, software licenses, cloud infrastructure costs, and vendor contracts. You will negotiate with vendors to get the best value for the company. You allocate resources effectively across different teams and projects. You justify budget requests to the CFO.
*   **Cross-Functional Collaboration:** Collaborate closely with Product, Design, Sales, and Marketing leaders to ensure engineering is aligned with business needs. You will manage stakeholder expectations regarding timelines and deliverables. You participate in executive meetings to represent the engineering perspective. You break down silos between departments.
*   **Talent Acquisition and Retention:** Oversee the recruiting strategy to attract top engineering talent. You will define the employer brand for engineering. You implement retention strategies to keep high performers, such as competitive compensation and growth opportunities. You ensure the interview process is rigorous and fair.
*   **Technical Governance:** Establish and enforce engineering standards, security policies, and compliance requirements. You will ensure that the organization adheres to best practices for data privacy (GDPR, CCPA) and security (SOC 2). You manage technical risk and ensure disaster recovery plans are in place. You have the final say on major architectural decisions.
*   **Innovation:** Encourage experimentation and the adoption of new technologies that can provide a competitive advantage. You will sponsor hackathons and R&D initiatives. You create space for engineers to explore new ideas. You balance innovation with stability.
*   **Communication:** Communicate engineering progress, challenges, and wins to the rest of the company. You will translate technical concepts into business language for non-technical stakeholders. You hold All-Hands meetings to keep the engineering team informed and aligned. You are the face of engineering.

### Role Variations

*   **CTO (Chief Technology Officer):** In smaller companies, the VP of Engineering and CTO roles might be combined. In larger ones, the CTO focuses on technical vision and R&D, while the VP of Engineering focuses on people, process, and execution. The VP of Eng reports to the CTO or CEO.
*   **Head of Engineering:** A title often used in mid-sized startups. Similar responsibilities to a VP but might be more hands-on with code or architecture initially. They scale the team from 10 to 50 engineers.
*   **Director of Engineering:** A level below VP, typically managing a specific vertical (e.g., Platform, Product) or a group of teams. The VP manages multiple Directors.
*   **VP of Platform Engineering:** Focuses specifically on the internal infrastructure and developer tools teams. They treat the rest of engineering as their customer.
*   **VP of Product Engineering:** Focuses specifically on the teams building customer-facing features. They work very closely with the CPO.

## Average Daily Tasks
*   **09:00 AM - Email and Slack Triage:** Review urgent messages and calendar invites. I check for any critical production incidents from the night before that require executive attention. I approve budget requests for new software tools.
*   **10:00 AM - Leadership Sync:** Weekly 1:1 with a Director of Engineering. We discuss the progress of the "Cloud Migration" project, potential hiring blockers, and the performance of a struggling manager. I provide coaching on how to handle a difficult conversation.
*   **11:00 AM - Executive Meeting:** Join the weekly executive team meeting (E-Staff). We discuss the company's Q3 goals. I give an update on the engineering department's progress towards the "99.99% Availability" OKR. I push back on a proposed deadline that would compromise quality.
*   **12:00 PM - Lunch with a Candidate:** Have lunch with a candidate for a Principal Engineer role. I sell them on the company vision and culture. I assess their cultural fit and leadership potential.
*   **01:00 PM - Strategy Planning:** Block out focus time to work on the H2 Engineering Strategy document. I analyze the current headcount and budget to determine if we can afford to spin up a new AI team. I draft a proposal for the board.
*   **02:30 PM - All-Hands Prep:** Review slides for the upcoming Engineering All-Hands. I want to highlight the team's recent success in reducing technical debt and announce the new promotion cycle. I ensure the tone is celebrating and motivating.
*   **03:00 PM - Cross-Functional Sync:** Meet with the VP of Product to align on the roadmap for the next quarter. We debate the trade-offs between shipping a new feature and stabilizing the platform. We come to a compromise on the scope.
*   **04:00 PM - Skip-Level Meeting:** Have a coffee chat with a Senior Engineer (skipping their manager). I ask about their experience working at the company, what could be improved, and if they have the tools they need. This helps me keep a pulse on the ground floor.
*   **04:30 PM - Recruiting Review:** Review the pipeline statistics with the Head of Talent. We notice a bottleneck in the onsite interview stage. We brainstorm ways to speed up the feedback loop. I approve an offer letter for a new hire.
*   **05:00 PM - Wrap-up:** Review my to-do list for tomorrow. I send a "kudos" email to a team that shipped a difficult feature today.

### Personalities

### Example Phrases
*   **Strategic Alignment:** "We need to align our technical roadmap with the company's Q4 revenue goals. If we're building features that don't drive retention or expansion, we're wasting resources. I want to review the top 3 priorities for each squad to ensure they map to our OKRs."
*   **Metrics Review:** "How are we tracking against our DORA metrics this month? I want to see the cycle time trend. If our deployment frequency is dropping, we need to investigate if we've introduced too much process or if the build pipeline is flaking. Speed is a proxy for health."
*   **Budget Approval:** "I'm approving the budget for the Kubernetes migration, but I need a clear timeline for the ROI. I need to explain to the CFO why this capital expenditure will reduce our operational costs in the long run. Let's set up a monthly review to track the savings."
*   **DEI Initiative:** "Let's focus on hiring for diversity in this next cohort; our pipeline needs work. We can't just wait for inbound applications. I want us to partner with organizations that support underrepresented groups in tech. This is a strategic imperative, not a nice-to-have."
*   **Stakeholder Management:** "I'll handle the communication with the Board regarding the delay; you just focus on the fix. I don't want the team worrying about the politics. Just give me the honest ETA and what you need to get it done. I've got your back."
*   **Cultural Reinforcement:** "I want to highlight the post-mortem from the outage last week. The team did an excellent job of being blameless and focusing on the systemic issue. This is exactly the kind of learning culture we want to build. Let's make sure those action items get prioritized."
*   **Career Coaching:** "Where do you see yourself in two years? Do you want to go down the management track or the individual contributor track? There is no wrong answer, but I want to make sure we're giving you the right opportunities to grow. Let's look at the career ladder together."
*   **Process Improvement:** "It feels like we have too many meetings. I'm declaring next Wednesday a 'No Meeting Day' for the entire engineering org. I want everyone to have uninterrupted deep work time. Let's see if that improves our velocity."
*   **Technical Debt:** "We need to dedicate 20% of our capacity to paying down technical debt. If we don't, our velocity will grind to a halt. I will defend this allocation to the product team. It's an investment in our future speed."
*   **Vision Setting:** "Imagine a world where a developer can ship code to production in 15 minutes with total confidence. That is our North Star for the platform team. Everything we build should move us closer to that reality."
