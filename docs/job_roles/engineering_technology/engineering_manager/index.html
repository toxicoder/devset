Engineering Manager

Role Code: SWEN0002

Job Description

A people-focused leadership role responsible for the health, happiness, and performance of a team of engineers. The Engineering Manager (EM) balances technical guidance with career coaching and project management. They ensure the team has clarity on their goals, unblock them when they get stuck, and help them grow in their careers. They are the first line of management in the engineering organization.

Responsibilities

  • People Management: Conduct weekly 1:1s, provide regular feedback, and write performance reviews. You will help your direct reports set goals and create development plans. You manage underperformance and recognize high performance. You care about the whole person, not just the employee.
  • Hiring and Onboarding: Actively participate in recruiting to build a diverse and high-performing team. You will write job descriptions, source candidates, and conduct interviews. You ensure new hires have a smooth onboarding experience and are productive quickly. You are constantly selling the team.
  • Team Health and Culture: Foster a psychological safe environment where team members feel comfortable taking risks and making mistakes. You will organize team building activities and celebrate wins. You mediate conflicts and ensure the team is cohesive. You set the tone for the team culture.
  • Delivery Management: Partner with the Product Manager to plan sprints and manage the backlog. You will ensure the team is delivering value at a sustainable pace. You help the team estimate work and manage dependencies. You remove blockers and shield the team from distractions.
  • Technical Guidance: While you may not write code daily, you have enough technical context to guide architectural decisions and trade-offs. You will ensure technical debt is managed and that the team follows engineering best practices. You ask the right questions to ensure the solution is sound.
  • Career Development: Help engineers navigate the career ladder, whether they want to become Staff Engineers or Managers. You will find opportunities for them to stretch their skills and get visibility. You advocate for their promotions during calibration meetings.
  • Process Improvement: continuously improve the team‚Äôs processes (e.g., Agile, Scrum, Kanban) to increase efficiency and quality. You will run retrospectives to identify areas for improvement. You ensure that meetings are effective and necessary.
  • Communication: Act as a conduit of information between the team and upper management. You will cascade company goals and strategy to the team. You communicate team status and risks to stakeholders. You ensure everyone is on the same page.
  • Stakeholder Management: Manage relationships with other teams (Design, QA, Sales) to ensure cross-functional alignment. You will negotiate timelines and scope with the PM. You represent the engineering team in cross-departmental meetings.
  • Budget and Resourcing: (At more senior levels) Manage the team‚Äôs budget for tools, training, and travel. You will plan for future headcount needs. You ensure the team has the resources they need to succeed.

Role Variations

  • The Technical Lead Manager (TLM): A hybrid role where the manager spends 50% of their time coding and 50% managing. Common in smaller teams or startups. They lead by example in the codebase.
  • The People-First Manager: Focuses almost exclusively on coaching, culture, and career growth. Often partners with a Staff Engineer who handles the technical leadership.
  • The Delivery Manager: Focuses heavily on project management, agile metrics, and execution. They run a tight ship and are obsessed with velocity.
  • The Fixer: Parachutes into struggling teams to turn them around. They focus on resolving conflict, fixing broken processes, and rebuilding trust.
  • The Scaling Manager: Specializes in taking a small team and growing it rapidly (e.g., doubling in size in a year). They focus on hiring pipelines and onboarding.
  • The Platform Manager: Manages an infrastructure or platform team. Their ‚Äúproduct‚Äù is internal developer tooling. Their stakeholders are other engineers.
  • The Product Manager: (Confusing title, but distinct role) Sometimes EMs act almost like PMs in technically complex domains (e.g., API teams).
  • The Remote Manager: Specializes in managing distributed teams. Focuses on async communication and documentation.
  • The Mentor Manager: Focuses on hiring junior talent and training them up. Their team is a ‚Äúbootcamp‚Äù for the rest of the org.
  • The Strategy Manager: Focuses on long-term technical strategy and roadmap planning. Works closely with Staff+ engineers.

Average Daily Tasks

  • 10:00 AM - Standup: Attend the team standup. I listen for blockers and mood. I don‚Äôt run the meeting (the team does), but I‚Äôm there to support.
  • 10:30 AM - 1:1 with Senior Engineer: Discuss their progress on the architecture RFC. I give feedback on their presentation style for the upcoming design review. We talk about their path to Staff Engineer.
  • 11:30 AM - Recruiting Sync: Meet with the recruiter to review the pipeline for the open Senior Backend role. We tweak the search criteria to find candidates with more distributed systems experience.
  • 12:00 PM - Lunch: Have lunch with a peer manager from another team to share challenges and learnings.
  • 01:00 PM - Sprint Planning: Join the team and the PM for sprint planning. I help the team push back on an unrealistic timeline. I ensure that we have allocated time for bug fixes.
  • 02:30 PM - 1:1 with Junior Engineer: They are struggling with a complex task. I ask coaching questions to help them unstuck themselves without giving them the answer. I reassure them that it‚Äôs okay to ask for help.
  • 03:30 PM - Performance Review Prep: Spend time writing the mid-year performance review for one of my reports. I gather feedback from their peers and look at their completed projects.
  • 04:30 PM - Tech Sync: Meeting with the Staff Engineer to discuss the technical debt backlog. We agree on which items to prioritize for the next quarter.
  • 05:00 PM - Wrap-up: Review my calendar for tomorrow. Send a quick note to the team celebrating a small win from today.
