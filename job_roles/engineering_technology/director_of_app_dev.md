# Director of App Dev

**Role Code:** SWEN0003

## Job Description
The Director of Application Development leads the software engineering teams responsible for building the company's customer-facing products. They oversee Backend, Frontend, and Mobile engineering disciplines, ensuring that they work in harmony to deliver high-quality software. They focus on feature delivery velocity, architectural integrity, and developer productivity. They act as a bridge between the technical teams and the product/business stakeholders.

## Responsibilities

*   **Product Delivery Strategy:** Partner closely with Product Management and Design to define the product roadmap and ensure engineering alignment. You will predict timelines, manage dependencies, and communicate risks to stakeholders. You are responsible for the "how" and "when" of product delivery. You ensure that the team is solving the right problems.
*   **Engineering Architecture:** Oversee the high-level technical architecture of the application ecosystem to ensure it is modular, scalable, and maintainable. You will review major technical design documents (RFCs) and guide the team away from anti-patterns. You balance short-term speed with long-term stability. You decide on the adoption of major new frameworks or languages.
*   **Technical Debt Management:** Champion the importance of technical health by balancing new feature development with refactoring and tech debt paydown. You will negotiate with product partners to reserve capacity for engineering-led initiatives. You ensure that the codebase remains a joy to work in. You prevent the "broken windows" effect.
*   **Developer Experience (DX):** Ensure that the tools, processes, and environments enable engineers to move fast with confidence. You will identify bottlenecks in the development lifecycle (e.g., slow builds, flaky tests) and resource teams to fix them. You measure and improve metrics like cycle time and deployment frequency. You strive for a frictionless development flow.
*   **Team Leadership and Growth:** Manage Engineering Managers and Staff Engineers, providing coaching, feedback, and career development support. You will define hiring plans and participate in key interviews to maintain a high bar for talent. You handle organizational design, deciding how to split or merge squads as the company grows. You foster a culture of ownership and excellence.
*   **Quality Assurance Strategy:** Define the strategy for software quality, including automated testing, manual QA, and observability. You will ensure that the team takes responsibility for the code they ship. You establish "Definition of Done" criteria. You drive initiatives to reduce the bug escape rate.
*   **Mobile Strategy:** specifically oversee the mobile engineering strategy (iOS and Android), ensuring parity with web features where appropriate and leveraging native capabilities. You will decide on cross-platform vs. native approaches. You manage the complexities of app store releases.
*   **Cross-Department Collaboration:** Work with Marketing, Sales, and Support to ensure that engineering outputs meet their needs. You will help technical support teams triage issues. You ensure that sales teams understand what is being built. You align engineering outputs with go-to-market strategies.
*   **Vendor Management:** Manage relationships with third-party software vendors and SaaS providers related to application development (e.g., CI tools, monitoring, specialized APIs). You will negotiate contracts and evaluate build vs. buy decisions. You keep costs in check.
*   **Incident Management:** Act as an escalation point for critical product incidents. You will ensure that customers are communicated with and that root cause analyses are conducted. You prioritize post-incident action items.

### Role Variations

*   **The Feature Factory Director:** Focused on high-velocity shipping. Metrics are number of PRs, features released, and cycle time. They optimize the organization for throughput. They work best in early-stage growth phases where market capture is everything. They are aggressive about deadlines.
*   **The Quality Director:** Obsessed with bugs, test coverage, and code reviews. "Zero bug policy." They slow down delivery to ensure stability. They work best in mature, high-stakes industries (FinTech, HealthTech). They implement rigorous gates.
*   **The Mobile/Frontend Specialist:** Background in UI/UX engineering. Deeply cares about pixel perfection and app performance. They push for design systems and smooth animations. They ensure the product "feels" premium.
*   **The Transformation Director:** Hired to fix a broken engineering organization. They focus on process, culture change, and restructuring. They turn around low-performing teams. They implement Agile/Scrum/Kanban from scratch.
*   **The API-First Director:** Focuses on building a robust platform for external developers. They treat the API as a product. They care deeply about documentation, versioning, and developer relations.
*   **The Enterprise Director:** Focuses on features needed for large enterprise clients (SSO, Audit Logs, RBAC). They work closely with the Sales team to unblock big deals. They understand long sales cycles.
*   **The Growth Director:** Partners with marketing to build experimentation platforms. They focus on A/B testing, funnel optimization, and viral loops. They are data-obsessed.
*   **The Legacy Modernizer:** Focuses on rewriting the monolith into microservices. They manage the complex migration process while keeping the business running. They are experts in the strangler fig pattern.
*   **The Remote-First Director:** Specializes in managing distributed, asynchronous teams across time zones. They focus on written communication and documentation. They build culture without an office.
*   **The "Player-Coach" Director:** In smaller orgs, they might still write code or architectural POCs. They lead by example in the IDE. They stay very close to the metal.

## Average Daily Tasks
*   **09:00 AM - Standup Review:** Briefly drop into a few squad standups to get a pulse on the team's energy and blockers. I don't run them, but I listen. I check Linear/Jira to see the sprint burn-down.
*   **10:00 AM - Product Roadmap Sync:** Meet with the VP of Product and Lead Designer to discuss the roadmap for Q3. We debate the feasibility of the "AI Assistant" feature. I push back on the timeline, citing necessary backend refactoring.
*   **11:00 AM - 1:1 with Engineering Manager:** Meet with one of my direct reports. We discuss a performance issue with a junior engineer on their team. I coach them on how to deliver constructive feedback. We also talk about their team's hiring needs.
*   **12:00 PM - Lunch:** Eat lunch, read tech news.
*   **01:00 PM - Architectural Review:** Join a "Principal Engineering Sync" where the Staff Engineers are discussing the new event-driven architecture. I ask questions about data consistency and operational complexity. I help break a tie decision.
*   **02:00 PM - Hiring/Recruiting:** Interview a candidate for a Senior Mobile Engineer role. I focus on system design and cultural add. Later, I review the pipeline with the internal recruiter to see why top-of-funnel is slow.
*   **03:00 PM - Incident Review:** Review the post-mortem from yesterday's outage. I ensure that the action items are actually scheduled and not just put in a backlog. I draft a brief email to the exec team explaining what happened in plain English.
*   **04:00 PM - Demo Review:** Attend the weekly "All-Hands Demo" where teams show off what they shipped. I ask questions and celebrate the wins. I look for UI inconsistencies or flow issues.
*   **05:00 PM - Release Planning:** Sync with the Release Manager to give the green light for the mobile app update. We check the crash-free user rate and beta feedback.
*   **05:30 PM - Strategy Work:** Spend the last 30 minutes drafting a proposal for "20% Time" to allow engineers to work on passion projects. I need to sell this to the CEO.

### Personalities
*   **The Shipper:** Loves clicking the "Release" button. Wants to see code in production. Metrics are number of PRs, features released, and cycle time. They get restless if nothing has shipped in a week.
*   **The Architect:** Thinks in systems. Microservices, APIs, Data Models. They ensure the pieces fit together. They draw boxes and arrows on whiteboards. They worry about coupling and cohesion.
*   **The Pragmatist:** Knows that "good enough" is often better than perfect. They optimize for business value. They prevent gold-plating. They focus on the MVP.
*   **The Defender:** Protects the team from scope creep. "That wasn't in the spec." They manage stakeholder expectations. They learn to say "No" or "Not now."
*   **The Modernizer:** Pushes for modern frameworks (React, GraphQL, Swift) to attract talent and improve DX. They want to work with cool tech. They read Hacker News.
*   **The Diplomat:** Navigates conflict between Product and Engineering. Finds the win-win. They translate between "dev speak" and "biz speak." They de-escalate tension.
*   **The Quality Hawk:** Hates bugs. Pushes for testing and QA. "Quality is free." They block releases that aren't ready. They celebrate bug bashes.
*   **The Talent Scout:** Always looking for the next great hire. Sells the vision. They know that great teams build great software. They spend time on LinkedIn.
*   **The Metrician:** Lives in dashboards. Jira velocity, DORA metrics, App Store ratings. They manage by numbers. They want to see the trend lines going up.
*   **The User Proxy:** Cares about the end-user experience. "This flow is confusing." They download the competitor's app. They act as the first beta tester.

### Example Phrases
*   **Decoupling Strategy:** "We need to decouple the frontend from the backend to iterate faster. Right now, every UI change requires a backend deploy, which is slowing us down. If we move to a Backend-for-Frontend (BFF) pattern or use GraphQL, the frontend team can move independently."
*   **Sprint Management:** "This sprint is overcommitted; what can we push to next week? We have 40 points of work and a velocity of 30. If we try to do it all, quality will suffer. I recommend moving the 'Dark Mode' tickets to the backlog for now."
*   **Risk Mitigation:** "Let's use a feature flag to roll this out gradually. I don't want to flip the switch for 100% of users on day one. Let's start with 5% (canary), monitor the error rates, and then ramp up. Safety first."
*   **Quality Priority:** "The mobile app crash rate is too high; prioritize fixes. We are at 98% crash-free users, and our goal is 99.9%. The App Store reviews are tanking. We need to stop feature work on the mobile squad for a week and just fix crashes."
*   **Contract First:** "We need to agree on the API contract before we start coding. If the backend and frontend teams start working based on assumptions, we'll have a nightmare integration phase. Let's write the Swagger/OpenAPI spec today and get it approved."
*   **Code Review Bottleneck:** "Code reviews are bottlenecking us; let's pair program. I see tickets sitting in 'Review' for 3 days. This increases our cycle time. If we pair, the review happens in real-time."
*   **Documentation:** "We need to document our component library. New hires are re-inventing the wheel because they don't know we already have a 'Button' component. Storybook needs to be our source of truth."
*   **API Design:** "Is this API REST or GraphQL? We need to be consistent. If we mix paradigms, the client code becomes a mess. I prefer GraphQL for this complex data requirement to avoid over-fetching."
*   **Critical Path:** "Let's focus on the critical path. The 'User Profile' features are nice, but if the 'Checkout' flow is broken, we make no money. Fix the payment gateway integration first."
*   **Deprecation:** "We need to deprecate this legacy endpoint. It's causing maintenance headaches and security risks. Let's analyze the traffic logs to see who is still using it and reach out to them."
