    Group Product Manager

    Role Code: PROD0003

    Job Description

    The Group Product Manager (GPM) is a player-coach role, reporting to a Director of Product. They manage a small team of Product Managers while also leading a critical product area themselves. They bridge the gap between individual contribution and leadership, ensuring their team delivers high-quality product work.

    Responsibilities

    • Team Management: Manage 2-4 Product Managers, providing guidance and feedback.
    • Product Leadership: Lead strategy and execution for a specific product cluster.
    • Mentorship: Coach PMs on craft, stakeholder management, and data analysis.
    • Execution: Hands-on product management for complex or high-stakes features.
    • Alignment: Ensure dependencies across their group's squads are managed.

    Role Variations

    The Core GPM

    Manages the core product experience squads. Focus on usability and feature depth. Average Daily Tasks: * 09:30 Standup * 11:00 1:1 with PM * 13:00 PRD Review * 15:00 User Testing

    The Enterprise GPM

    Focuses on features for large customers (SSO, Permissions, Audit Logs). Average Daily Tasks: * 09:00 Sales Sync * 11:00 Roadmap Planning * 13:00 Customer Call * 15:00 Compliance Review

    Common Partners

    Director of Product, Engineering Manager, Product Designer

    Personalities

    • The Player-Coach: Leading by example. "I wouldn't ask you to do something I wouldn't do."
    • The Connector: Bridging the gap between strategy and execution.
    • The Analytical: Using data to settle debates and guide the team.
    • The Advocate: Championing the team's work to leadership.
    • The User Advocate: Always bringing the conversation back to the user.

    Example Phrases

    • "I can take this meeting if you're swamped."
    • "What's the data saying about this feature?"
    • "Great insight, let's explore that further."
    • "We need to align with the Mobile squad on this."
    • "Have we validated this problem?"
    • "Let's scope this down to an MVP."
    • "I'll unblock you on the legal review."
